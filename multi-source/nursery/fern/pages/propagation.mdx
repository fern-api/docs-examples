---
title: Propagation
subtitle: Techniques for multiplying plant stock.
---

Propagation lets the nursery multiply plant stock without sourcing additional seeds. The system tracks propagation batches alongside seed-grown plants so inventory stays accurate.

## Supported methods

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Stem cuttings">
Take a 10–15 cm cutting below a node, remove lower leaves, and place in a rooting medium. Log the cutting with `POST /nursery/propagation` using method `stem_cutting`.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Division">
Split an established root clump into sections, each with at least one growing point. Use method `division` when logging.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Layering">
Pin a low-growing branch to the soil surface and wait for roots to form at the contact point. Use method `layering` when logging.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Tracking batches

Every propagation event creates a batch linked to the parent plant. Query batches with `GET /nursery/propagation?parent_plant_id={plantId}` to see rooting success rates and timeline.
